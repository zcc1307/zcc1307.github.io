About Me

Hello! I am an assistant professor at University of Arizona I am a postdoctoral researcher at Microsoft Research NYC. I got my PhD in Computer Science at UCSD, where I was lucky to have Prof. Kamalika Chaudhuri as my advisor. Before that, I was an undergraduate student in Department of Machine Intelligence, School of EECS, Peking University, China, where I spent a great time studying machine learning theory with Prof. Liwei Wang. In Summer 2015, I was a research intern at Yahoo Labs NYC, mentored by Dr. Alina Beygelzimer. In Summer 2016, I did a second internship at Yahoo Research NYC, working with Dr. Alina Beygelzimer and Dr. Francesco Orabona.

Research

My research interests lie in both theory and applications of machine learning. I primarily work on interactive learning (e.g. active learning, online learning, etc) and confidence-rated prediction, designing practical algorithms as well as formalizing their theoretical guarantees.
